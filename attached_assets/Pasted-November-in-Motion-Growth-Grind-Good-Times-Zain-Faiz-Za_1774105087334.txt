What I worked on this month
This month, I worked on my Replicate, which was a MNIST handwritten digit recognition project built with Python and TensorFlow/Keras. The goal was to train a neural network to recognize digits (0–9) from the MNIST dataset. I completed the full build and training, added visualizations like accuracy/loss plots and a confusion matrix, and now the model can take input images and output predictions, though it occasionally misclassifies some digits.

Thanks for reading Zain’s Substack! Subscribe for free to receive new posts and support my work.

Working on Replicate helped me gain hands-on experience with data preprocessing, model training, and evaluating performance, while improving my Python and machine learning skills. Completing it was a major milestone in applying AI concepts practically and showed me the challenges and rewards of building a fully functional ML model from scratch.

She also talked about how careers and passions can evolve, and how learning and mentorship don’t need to be limited by location. It was inspiring to see how she combines her expertise with empathy to make a real impact, which made me think about how science can be used to help people in meaningful ways.

A talk by Valentyn Volkov

On November 25th, I had the chance to attend a talk by Valentyn Volkov, the founder of XPANCEO, the first deep-tech unicorn company in the UAE. It’s not every day you get to see the future of human augmentation up close.

My reflection on this Month
Looking back at November, it was honestly a mix of chaos, progress, and a lot of learning. Between exams, field trips, sports days, and all the TKS talks, every day felt full, sometimes exhausting, but always valuable. I pushed through my replicate project, and even when things didn’t go perfectly, I learned how to manage time, priorities, and energy better.

The talks and experiences this month gave me a lot to think about — about mindset, responsibility, and using my skills to actually make an impact. I feel like I ended the month more focused, more aware of how I want to approach my goals, and more inspired to take action rather than just plan. Overall, November reminded me that even when life gets hectic, growth happens when you stay engaged, stay curious, and push yourself a little further every day.

I also realised the importance of balance — between work and rest, kindness and self-respect, productivity and fun. Some days were physically and mentally draining, but taking moments to enjoy time with friends, reflect, or just reset made a huge difference. November taught me that progress isn’t always about doing everything perfectly; it’s about showing up, learning from each experience, and keeping momentum, even through the messy, unpredictable days.

Mindsets of this month
1. Productivity & Grind — This month, I pushed through exams, replicate work, and outreach despite a packed schedule. I implemented this by setting daily goals, staying focused during study blocks, and keeping momentum even when tired or distracted.

2. Growth & Learning — Inspired by the TKS talks and new experiences, I focused on learning from each situation. Whether it was a virtual session on AI, functional nutrition, or mindset, I reflected on the key lessons and applied them to my projects and daily habits.

3. Boss Mentality — Stepping up during Sports Day and other responsibilities, I took charge instead of waiting for others. I planned teams, made decisions quickly, and led by example, showing that leadership is about action and ownership.

4. Balance & Self-Respect — Between school, TKS, and social activities, I realized the importance of balancing work, rest, and relationships. I practiced this by intentionally taking breaks, spending time with friends, and setting boundaries to maintain energy and focus.

5. Accountability & Discipline — From replicate progress to outreach emails, I held myself accountable to my goals. I tracked tasks, reviewed progress nightly, and reminded myself that showing up consistently matters more than perfection.

6. Curiosity & Open-Mindedness — Attending talks encouraged me to stay curious and embrace new ideas. I implemented this by actively asking questions, reflecting on new perspectives, and exploring how I could integrate insights into my own work.

These mindsets weren’t just ideas — they shaped how I approached November, helping me stay productive, lead effectively, and learn actively even during a hectic month.
