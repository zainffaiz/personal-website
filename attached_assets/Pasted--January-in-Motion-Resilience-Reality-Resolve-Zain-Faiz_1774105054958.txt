Monthly Newsletter

What I worked on this month

This month, I’ve been working on a project at TKS in collaboration with Lovable. It’s been intense, with weekly team syncs, outreach for validation, research and interviews. The submission isn’t until February 14th, so we’re still deep in the process, but the work has been rewarding. Coordinating with my team, conducting interviews to validate our ideas, and turning research into actionable solutions has taught me a lot about moving from concept to execution.

Outside of that, I took on a major role in our school garden project. We’re growing crops like tomatoes, radish, mint, carrots, and more, with plans for a harvest later on. I’ve been overseeing almost everything, helping with the actual gardening, planning the layout, including pipes and fountain designs, and leading fundraising efforts. We ran a donations stall during parent-teacher meetings, which was a huge win and will help us afford what we need to make real progress.

Between the challenge work, school responsibilities, and managing everything in between, January pushed me to show up consistently even when things got difficult.

Events I attended

Workshop Mania

On January 3rd, TKS kicked off the year with Workshop Mania, a full day of back-to-back workshops led by TKS Directors and special guests. I attended everyone which i could possibly attend (I had to skip some because the timings overlapped), and two stood out the most.

The Olympian Mindset with Charlotte Fairbank showed me how elite athletes build discipline, resilience, and competitive edge, and how those same principles transfer directly to life. It reminded me that consistency and mental toughness matter more than bursts of motivation.

How People Actually Get Rich with Skylor gave a systems-level breakdown of how wealth is really built and how to position yourself early. It shifted my perspective on thinking long-term and understanding leverage.

The other workshops I attended covered everything from building investing instincts to writing better, reflecting on the past year, landing internships, and validating ideas with real users. The day was packed, and even though some sessions overlapped and I couldn’t attend everything, it set a strong tone for how I wanted to approach the rest of the month.

Founder Panel

Later in the month, I attended a panel moderated by my friend Haifa, featuring founders and innovators discussing the journey from idea to impact. The conversation covered the founder mindset, like curiosity, resilience, and ownership, but also acknowledged something that often gets overlooked: the founder journey can be a lonely one.

What stuck with me most was the discussion around competition. Instead of seeing it as a threat, the panel framed it as a force that sharpens focus, drives differentiation, and strengthens execution. In crowded rooms, it can be hard to find your voice, but there’s always room for you if you carve out your unique space with courage, authenticity, and perseverance.

The session reinforced the importance of validation, ensuring there’s real demand for an idea before investing too much in it, and reminded me that even when the path feels isolating, showing up and iterating is what moves you forward.

Stories, Experiences & Lessons Learned

January was tough. There’s no way around it. It wasn’t a month of smooth progress or easy wins. It was a month that tested me emotionally and mentally in ways I really didn’t expect.

Between managing school responsibilities, leading the garden project, working through the Lovable challenge, and navigating personal challenges at home, I found myself stretched thin. Some days I felt like I was just surviving, not thriving. But even in those moments, I kept showing up. I kept doing the work, even when I didn’t feel like it.

Become a Medium member
One thing that stood out this month was realizing that progress doesn’t always feel good while it’s happening. Sometimes it’s uncomfortable. Sometimes it’s exhausting. But it still counts. Quiet effort, even when no one sees it, compounds over time.

I also learned the importance of asking for help and leaning on the people around me. Whether it was my team during syncs, my friends during tough days, or my family supporting me through everything, I realized I don’t have to carry everything alone.

January taught me that resilience isn’t about being strong all the time. It’s about showing up even when things are hard, and trusting that you’ll come out the other side better for it.

Reflection of this month

Looking back, January felt heavy. It wasn’t the calm, reflective reset that December was, and it wasn’t the chaotic but exciting energy of November. It was something in between, a month that demanded more from me than I expected.

There were moments where I questioned if I was doing enough, if I was managing everything well, or if I should be handling things differently. But in those moments, I reminded myself that growth doesn’t always look like progress. Sometimes it looks like endurance. Sometimes it’s just about not giving up.

What stood out most was realizing that tough months build character. December taught me the value of calm consistency. November taught me how to grind through chaos. January taught me how to keep going even when things get difficult, even when the path isn’t clear, and even when the weight feels heavy.

I’m proud of how I showed up this month. Not because everything went perfectly, but because I didn’t let the challenges stop me from moving forward.

Mindsets of this month

Resilience Through Adversity
Resilience isn’t about avoiding hard moments, it’s about moving through them. January reinforced that strength comes from showing up even when it’s difficult, and trusting that endurance builds character over time.

Execution Over Perfection
Between the Lovable challenge, school work, and the garden project, I learned that taking action and iterating matter more than waiting for the perfect plan. Progress happens when you start, not when everything is ready.

Lean on Your People
You don’t have to carry everything alone. Whether it’s your team, your friends, or your family, the people around you are there to support you. Asking for help isn’t a weakness — it’s how you keep moving forward.

Validation Before Investment
Before committing fully to an idea, make sure there’s real demand for it. Whether it’s through outreach, interviews, or testing, validation saves time and ensures you’re solving a problem that matters.

Endurance Compounds
Not every day will feel productive or meaningful, but showing up consistently — even on the hard days — creates momentum. Small efforts add up, and endurance over time leads to real growth.

These mindsets shaped how I navigated January. It wasn’t an easy month, but it was a month that mattered. Going into February, I’m carrying these lessons with me.
