What I worked on this month
This month, I spent one week participating in a mini-challenge by XPANCEO for TKS. It was short but intense, pushing me to stay accountable, work with a team, and manage my time more effectively. I’ll talk more about that experience later in this newsletter.

Outside of that, I focused on showing up consistently, staying active, and keeping routines that helped me reset and stay grounded.

I also focused on my mental game, staying calm under pressure and reflecting on small wins. The details are in my reflections later in this newsletter.

Events I attended
Demo Day at TKS

On December 7th, we had our Demo Day at The Knowledge Society, and it was one of those moments where you finally slow down and see how far you’ve come. At first, I was nervous, my hands were shaky, and my mind was racing, but as I began presenting, I started calming down and actually got into it.

By the end, it didn’t even feel like presenting anymore. I got to share my work, connect with people about what I care about, and talk about my future goals in a way that felt real and exciting. I also spent time checking out my friends’ projects, and seeing their effort, creativity, and perseverance was genuinely inspiring.

The day gave me a mix of joy, freedom, and a sense of accomplishment. It reminded me that growth isn’t just about pushing projects forward — it’s also about celebrating what you’ve done, sharing it with others, and learning from the energy around you.

Turning Ideas Into Action with Farza Majeed

Press enter or click to view image in full size

Farza Majeed visited TKS on December 14th to share his journey and insights. He walked us through his experience creating an AR Menu app and explained how overcomplicating ideas can actually slow progress. The key lesson he shared was the concept of “toy versions,” small, simple versions of your ideas meant to be tested, shown to people, and iterated on based on feedback. This approach flips hesitation into action and helps ideas grow faster than waiting for perfection.

Hearing Farza’s story made me reflect on how often I refine ideas before even testing them, and reminded me that progress comes from building, learning, and iterating. It was a powerful reminder that movement, no matter how small, creates growth.

TKS partnered with Xpanceo to create a ‘mini-challenge’ that lasted a week, stretching from 13th December to 20th December. The challenge itself was fast-paced, but what really made it memorable was how my team and I worked together. We coordinated seamlessly, bouncing ideas off each other, splitting tasks, and constantly adjusting as new problems came up.

During the challenge, I realized how much invisible work goes into collaboration, planning, thinking ahead, and communicating clearly without making it feel like effort. Even small decisions, like who tackled which task or how we tested our ideas, compounded into bigger progress by the end of each day. It wasn’t about flashy results; it was about showing up, moving together as a team, and learning as we went.

This week reminded me that consistency, focus, and teamwork can make even short, intense challenges feel like a huge growth opportunity.

Stories, Experiences & Lessons Learned
December gave me space to slow down and actually pay attention to how I was moving through the month. Instead of jumping from one thing to the next, I found myself noticing small shifts, how I responded to challenges, how I managed my time and energy, and how often progress showed up quietly rather than in obvious wins.

Looking back, a lot of what mattered most wasn’t tied to specific outcomes or moments, but to how consistently I showed up and how intentional I became with my actions. The month reminded me that growth doesn’t always feel exciting while it’s happening, but it becomes clear when you pause long enough to reflect.

This month taught me that invisible work compounds. Even when nothing dramatic is happening, something meaningful usually is.

Reflection of this month
Looking back, December felt noticeably calmer compared to the months before it. It wasn’t a month of big spikes or dramatic moments, but more of a reset, a chance to slow down and reflect on how I was actually spending my time and energy. In that quieter pace, I became more aware of my habits, my mindset, and the small decisions that usually go unnoticed day to day.

What stood out most was realizing that growth doesn’t always come from intensity or pressure. Sometimes it comes from consistency, reflection, and simply showing up with intention. December reminded me that progress can be subtle, and that these calm periods are often what set the foundation for bigger moves later on.

Mindsets of this month
Quiet Consistency
Consistency doesn’t always need to be loud. Small, repeated actions done quietly over time matter more than short bursts of intensity.

Momentum
December reinforced that progress comes from staying in motion, not forcing results. Even slow movement forward still counts.

Reflection
Taking time to observe what’s working and what isn’t makes growth more intentional and adjustments more meaningful.

These mindsets shaped how I moved through December. November was chaotic and fast; December was calm and thoughtful. Going into January, I’m taking both with me.
